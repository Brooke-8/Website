Chameleon

Chameleon is a web-app that I created together with Rebecca Beagley, Rachel Q and Steven Wu, as part of the uOttaHack 7 Hackathon. The idea behind Chameleon was to help artists and designers quickly and easily extract visually-appealing colour palettes from images. We wanted something that would not just take the most common colours in the image, but that would consider the colours together as a set to choose the most aesthetically pleasing combination that represents the image.

We wanted using Chameleon to be as simple as possible. All you need to do to generate an image is upload a file from your computer with the Browse Files button, and from there it will automatically generate the palette from the image. You can then change the number of colours that are generated by inputting a number in the Number of Colours box. Clicking Regenerate Palette will quickly generate another palette based on the image so that you can quickly cycle through options until you find one to your liking. Afterwards you can simply click on the colours below to copy the hex codes to your keyboard.

The app was made on Gadget, a Javascript development and hosting platform that uses React. The generation of the colour palettes is done by calling OpenAI's Vision API.
